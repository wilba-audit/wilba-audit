## Design Decisions

### Key Decisions Made

1. **Stored in `outputs/brand/`** — This is a deliverable Jess will reference repeatedly, not a template or a one-off. A `brand/` subdirectory under `outputs/` keeps brand strategy assets together as more are created (voice guide, visual identity brief, etc.).

2. **4 audience segments, not 2 or 10** — Fewer than 4 risks over-generalising. More than 4 is too many to hold in mind while creating content. The 4 segments map directly to WILBA's target sectors: coaches, service-based SMBs, real estate, and hospitality/retreats.

3. **Psychology-first structure** — Most content strategy docs start with demographics. This one starts with psychology (frustrations → emotional triggers → desires) because that's what actually informs great content. Demographics are secondary.

4. **Platform-specific sections** — Instagram and YouTube require completely different content approaches. The document treats them as separate channels with separate strategies, not one strategy copy-pasted across both.

5. **30-day calendar as the final section** — Strategy without activation is just theory. A ready-to-use 30-day calendar removes the activation energy barrier and gives Jess something to hand off to a VA or execute herself immediately.

6. **Written from the POV of a senior social media strategist** — The document is written as a professional strategic brief. Not a checklist. It explains the *why* behind each recommendation so Jess can adapt it as she learns what works.

### Alternatives Considered

- **Separate documents per segment** — rejected, harder to see cross-segment patterns and connections
- **Storing in `reference/`** — rejected, `reference/` is for reusable templates/patterns; this is a specific deliverable for Jess's brand
- **Including competitor analysis** — scoped out for now; a second document can profile specific competitors once Jess has identified her top 3

### Open Questions

None — ready to implement.
