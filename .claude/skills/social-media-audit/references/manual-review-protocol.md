# Manual Review Protocol — auditing the REAL content

Use this when you have access to the actual content (via the
`social-data-collector` output, analytics screenshots, or the client sharing
posts). This is the forensic layer that turns a strategic diagnosis into a
grounded, post-by-post audit. Never score retention/hooks as "firm" without
having done this pass on real posts.

## Inputs you want in hand
- `collected-data.md` from the collector (real profile stats + recent posts), and/or
- Native analytics screenshots (last 90 days), and/or
- Links to 10 recent posts you can actually watch.

## Step 1 — Profile-as-cold-visitor test (60 seconds)
Open the profile as if you're the ICP seeing it for the first time. Score:
- [ ] In 3 seconds, is it clear who this is FOR and the outcome offered? (not credentials)
- [ ] Is there ONE obvious next step (link/CTA)?
- [ ] Does the grid/feed signal a clear, repeated theme, or is it scattered?
Record the exact bio text and verdict.

## Step 2 — Per-post teardown (do 8–12 recent posts)
For each post, fill one row. This is where hook/retention/value scores come from.

| Field | What to capture |
|-------|-----------------|
| Link / date | |
| Format | Reel / carousel / long-form / Short / text |
| **Hook (first 2s)** | Transcribe the literal opening words + on-screen text. Verdict: promise/tension in frame 1? Or logo/"hi I'm…"/slow set-up? |
| Topic & ICP fit | Is it aimed at the patient or at peers? Jargon level. |
| Value density | Does it teach something specific only she can? Or generic advice? |
| CTA | What's the next step? Save/send? Link? None? |
| **Public metrics** | Views, likes, comments, and (if available) saves/shares + watch %. |
| Comment quality | ICP/patients asking real questions? Peers? Bots? Silence? |
| Verdict | Why this post did what the numbers say it did. |

## Step 3 — Pattern extraction
Across the rows, compute/observe:
- **Retention proxy:** if watch % unavailable, use views-vs-follower ratio and
  comment depth as proxies; state it's a proxy.
- **Top 3 vs bottom 3:** what do winners share (hook type, topic, format)? What
  do losers share? This is the single most actionable output — it tells her what
  to make more of.
- **Cadence reality:** actual posting dates → gaps, bursts, or steady rhythm.
- **Format mix:** is she over-invested in the format that's underperforming
  (usually produced Reels) and under-invested in the one that compounds
  (usually YouTube long-form / carousels)?
- **Distribution footprint:** same content cross-posted? Any email/collab/PR? Or post-once?

## Step 4 — Map findings to the rubric & failure patterns
Now score the 8 dimensions (audit-framework.md) with evidence per dimension,
and tag each weakness with a pattern from failure-patterns.md, citing the
specific posts as proof. A firm score cites at least 2 real posts.

## Step 5 — Distinguish real vs. still-needed
Every figure in the final audit is tagged:
- **[REAL]** — observed from collected data / analytics.
- **[PROXY]** — inferred from a public signal; say which.
- **[CONFIRM]** — not obtainable publicly; needs her analytics.
Honesty about the source is what separates a WILBA audit from a guess.
