# Proposal Template — honest, tiered, outcome-anchored

Structure every WILBA proposal like this. Price to **outcome + bandwidth
returned**, never to "number of videos." Honesty closes: include a "What we
won't promise" section — it disarms scepticism from someone who's been burned.

---

## 1. Cover / one-line promise
Who it's for + the transformation. Not "social media management" — e.g.
"Turning your expertise into the reason the right patients choose you."

## 2. What we heard (mirror the client)
2–3 sentences proving you listened: their spend, their frustration, their goal.
Name the burn without blame ("you invested in beautiful video and it didn't
travel — that's not your fault, and it's fixable").

## 3. What we found (the audit, distilled)
- The weighted score + the one-line metaphor.
- Top 3 root causes (from failure-patterns.md), each with the concrete evidence.
- The 1–2 genuine strengths (there are always some — credibility, footage, face).
Keep it honest and specific. This section builds the trust the price rests on.

## 4. The opportunity (prognosis, with ranges + dates)
What's realistically achievable and when. Leading indicators you control, not
follower promises. Explicitly: authority compounds over 6–12 months.

## 5. The plan (tiers — let them choose commitment)
Present three tiers so a "not decided" client self-selects:

### Tier 1 — Audit & Strategy Sprint  (entry / low-risk)
- Deliverables: full audit (this doc), ICP + positioning, 90-day content
  strategy, content-pillar system, hook bank, platform plan, 30-day calendar.
- Client keeps the roadmap even if they produce elsewhere.
- Price: fixed one-off. Position as "de-risk before you spend another pound on video."

### Tier 2 — Content Engine  (done-for-you production)
- WILBA's AI pipeline (Perplexity → Script → ElevenLabs → HeyGen → CreatorMate)
  + human strategy. Structured monthly interview to capture the client's real POV.
- Deliverables/month: X long-form + Y short-form + Z carousels, all repurposed
  across platforms, scheduled, with monthly reporting on goal metrics.
- Price: monthly retainer. Anchor to bandwidth returned + cost-per-asset vs their
  previous production spend.

### Tier 3 — Full Growth Partner
- Everything in Tier 2 + distribution (email, collabs, PR outreach), community
  management, conversion-path build (lead magnet, email nurture), quarterly strategy.
- Price: higher retainer. For when they want the outcome owned end-to-end.

Recommend one tier explicitly, with the reason. For a "not decided" client,
recommend starting at **Tier 1** — it's honest (proves value before big spend),
low-risk for them, and a natural on-ramp to Tier 2/3.

## 6. Why WILBA (not another production house)
The differentiator: **production houses sell polish; WILBA sells traction.** We
engineer the hook, the retention, the ICP fit and the distribution — the things
that actually move the numbers — and we capture *your* real voice, not generic
agency gloss. AI pipeline = more volume, faster, at lower cost per asset.

## 7. What we won't promise (the honesty section — keep it)
- We won't promise virality or a follower number. Anyone who does is guessing.
- We won't sacrifice your clinical/professional integrity for reach.
- We will promise a system, leading indicators we control, and monthly truth on
  what's working. If a channel isn't earning its keep in 90 days, we'll tell you
  and change course.

## 8. Investment & next step
Clear pricing table, what's included, start date, and ONE simple next action
(e.g. "reply 'go' and we book the kickoff interview this week").

---

### Pricing logic (internal — don't show the client)
- Tier 1 must be genuinely valuable standalone AND priced to be an easy yes.
- Tier 2/3 anchor against their *previous* per-video spend to make volume look
  like the bargain it is.
- Never discount by cutting strategy — cut volume instead. Strategy is the value.
- Leave margin for the developer-partner's fulfilment cost.
