# LinkedIn Review Module

LinkedIn is a different game from the short-form platforms and deserves its own
lens — especially for an expert/authority client (doctors, consultants, B2B).
For Dr Schoeman it reaches the *highest-value* audiences: referring clinicians,
corporate-wellness buyers, journalists, and affluent professional patients.

## Why LinkedIn matters for an authority client
- Best organic reach-per-follower of any platform for professional content.
- The audience has money and decision-making power (referrals, partnerships, PR).
- Authority here compounds into real-world credibility (speaking, press, B2B deals)
  that consumer platforms don't reach.

## What to review (public + profile)
1. **Profile as a landing page** (not a CV):
   - [ ] Headline: is it a patient/audience promise or a job title? ("Helping women reclaim energy through hormone & longevity medicine" beats "Functional Medicine Doctor at The Schoeman Clinic").
   - [ ] Banner + about: do they speak to the reader's problem, or list credentials?
   - [ ] Featured section: is her best content/lead magnet pinned? Any CTA to the clinic?
   - [ ] Activity: is she actually posting, or is it a static résumé?
2. **Content (if posting):** apply the per-post teardown (manual-review-protocol.md),
   but weight for LinkedIn's ranking signals below.

## How LinkedIn ranks content (2026)
- **Early engagement from her network + dwell time** drive reach; comments weigh
  heavily (a thoughtful comment > a like).
- **Native beats outbound:** text posts, carousels ("documents"), and native
  video outperform posts that push people off-platform with a link. Put links in
  the first comment, not the post body.
- **Consistency + genuine POV** win. Reposted Reels with hashtag walls read as
  spam and get suppressed.

## The authority play for a doctor here
- **Clinical POV posts:** a myth she debunks weekly ("No, 'normal' bloods don't
  mean your hormones are fine — here's why").
- **Anonymised, consented patient-outcome stories** (compliance-checked): the
  most powerful trust-builder on the platform.
- **Document carousels** teaching one concept (high dwell, saved, shared internally).
- **Commentary on research/news** in her field — positions her as the interpreter.
- Goal metric here is NOT reach — it's *comment quality from the right people*
  and inbound (referrals, press, partnership DMs).

## Compliance note
Same guardrails as all health content: educational framing over promotional,
no unverified claims, testimonials only where GMC/ASA-permitted and consented.
On LinkedIn the professional audience makes over-claiming especially costly to
credibility — precision and restraint read as authority here.

## Scoring note
Fold LinkedIn into the main scorecard, but call it out separately in the audit:
often an authority client's biggest *untapped* channel is exactly here — high
reach potential, high-value audience, and usually the most neglected.
