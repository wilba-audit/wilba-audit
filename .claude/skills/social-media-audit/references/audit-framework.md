# Audit Framework — the 8-dimension rubric

Score each dimension **0–5**. Show the score, one line of evidence, and the fix.
Then compute a **weighted total** using the goal weights below, so the headline
number reflects what *this* client actually needs.

## Scoring anchors (apply to every dimension)
- **0** — Absent / actively harmful.
- **1** — Present but broken; working against the goal.
- **2** — Below standard; obvious gaps a novice would spot.
- **3** — Competent baseline; nothing wrong, nothing special.
- **4** — Strong; better than most accounts in the niche.
- **5** — Best-in-class; a genuine competitive moat.

Most "spent money, no traction" accounts score 2–3 on production but 0–2 on
positioning, hooks, ICP and distribution. That gap *is* the story.

## The 8 dimensions

| # | Dimension | The question it answers | Common failure |
|---|-----------|------------------------|----------------|
| 1 | Positioning & profile | In 3 seconds, is it clear who this is for and why they're *the* authority? | Bio is a CV, not a promise. No niche. |
| 2 | ICP clarity | Is content aimed at ONE person, or "everyone"? | Talks to peers/everyone → resonates with no one. |
| 3 | Hook & retention | Do the first 2 seconds earn the next 10? Does watch-time hold? | Slow branded intros; production gloss ≠ hook. |
| 4 | Content–format–platform fit | Right message, right format, right platform? | Long lecture cut into Reels; wrong platform entirely. |
| 5 | Value density & differentiation | Does each post teach something only THEY can? | Generic advice anyone could post. |
| 6 | Consistency & volume | Enough reps for the algorithm to learn the account? | Feast-or-famine; big launches then silence. |
| 7 | Distribution & repurposing | One shoot → how many touches? Anything off-platform? | Post once, pray. No email, no collabs, no PR. |
| 8 | Conversion path | Does a viewer have an obvious next step to the real offer? | Great content, dead-end. No CTA, no capture. |

## Goal weights (multiply each dimension score by its weight, sum, normalise to 100)

| Dimension | Authority | Lead gen | Growth | Sales |
|-----------|:--------:|:-------:|:-----:|:----:|
| 1 Positioning | 3 | 3 | 2 | 3 |
| 2 ICP clarity | 3 | 3 | 2 | 3 |
| 3 Hook & retention | 2 | 2 | 3 | 2 |
| 4 Format/platform fit | 2 | 2 | 3 | 2 |
| 5 Value & differentiation | 3 | 2 | 2 | 2 |
| 6 Consistency & volume | 2 | 2 | 3 | 2 |
| 7 Distribution & repurposing | 2 | 3 | 3 | 2 |
| 8 Conversion path | 1 | 3 | 1 | 3 |

**Weighted score = Σ(dimension score × goal weight) ÷ Σ(goal weight × 5) × 100.**
Report as a % with a one-line interpretation:
- 0–39% — Foundational problems; production spend is being wasted. Stop, fix, restart.
- 40–59% — Real potential, leaking at 2–3 points. High-leverage fixes available.
- 60–79% — Solid base; optimisation and consistency will compound.
- 80–100% — Strong; scale distribution and defend the moat.

## Metrics that matter, by goal (judge outcomes, not vanity)

- **Authority:** saves + shares per post, comment quality (peers/ICP vs bots),
  profile-visit→follow %, "sends" (DM shares), branded-search lift, inbound DM quality.
- **Lead gen:** qualified enquiries, cost per booked call, link CTR, DM→consult rate.
- **Growth:** % reach to *non-followers*, follow-rate per reach, net growth %/wk.
- **Sales:** link clicks → checkout/booking conversion, revenue attributable to social.

Vanity metrics (views, follower count in isolation, likes) go in a footnote, not
the headline — unless the goal is literally growth.

## How to present the score
Never hand over a naked number. Format: **"58% — Real potential, leaking at
three points. You're paying for a Ferrari engine (production) bolted to a car
with no steering (positioning) and no fuel line (distribution). Fix those two
and the same footage performs 3–5×."** The metaphor carries the maths.
