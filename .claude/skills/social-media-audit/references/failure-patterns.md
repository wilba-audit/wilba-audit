# Failure-Pattern Library — why content doesn't get traction

Each pattern: the tell, the root cause, the fix. When you diagnose, name the
pattern so the client sees it's a known, solvable problem — not personal failure.

### 1. The Production-Value Fallacy ★ (the #1 cause of "spent money, no traction")
**Tell:** high-budget, cinematic video; low reach and watch-time.
**Root cause:** budget went to gloss (crew, lighting, edit) instead of the two
things that move the algorithm — the **hook** and **retention structure**.
Platforms rank watch-through, not spend. A £15k film and a phone selfie compete
on the same metric.
**Fix:** reallocate budget from polish to *strategy, scripting, hooks, volume*.
The same on-camera talent, scripted for retention and shot at 5× the volume,
outperforms a handful of expensive films.

### 2. No Hook System
**Tell:** videos open with a logo sting, "Hi, I'm…", or a slow scene-set.
**Root cause:** treating video as broadcast TV. The first 2 seconds are the
whole ballgame; a 3s intro is a 3s exit ramp.
**Fix:** open on the payoff/tension. On-screen text promise in frame 1. Build a
hook bank; test 3 hooks per idea.

### 3. Talking to Peers, Not the ICP
**Tell:** clinically precise, jargon-heavy, impresses colleagues, loses patients.
**Root cause:** expert's curse — creating for people like themselves.
**Fix:** write to ONE named patient and their 2am Google search. Translate every
term. Authority with the audience, not the medical board.

### 4. No ICP ("everyone" content)
**Tell:** topics sprawl; no consistent through-line; "wellness for all."
**Root cause:** fear of narrowing. But broad = invisible; the algorithm can't
classify the account, and no viewer feels "that's *me*."
**Fix:** pick the one person and the one transformation. Narrow to grow.

### 5. Feast-or-Famine Cadence
**Tell:** a burst of posts around a launch, then weeks of silence.
**Root cause:** treating content as campaigns, not a system.
**Fix:** sustainable cadence the algorithm can learn from (e.g. 3–4 Reels/wk +
1 long-form/wk). Consistency > intensity. Batch to survive real life.

### 6. Post-Once-and-Pray (no distribution/repurposing)
**Tell:** one asset, one post, one platform. Nothing off-platform.
**Root cause:** thinking creation = distribution. Making content is 20% of the job.
**Fix:** one shoot → 8–12 touches across platforms + email + carousels + collabs.
Build an email list you *own* (reach you don't rent). Pursue collabs/PR for borrowed audiences.

### 7. Dead-End Content (no conversion path)
**Tell:** good content, but a viewer has no obvious next step to the real offer.
**Root cause:** content and business treated as separate.
**Fix:** every account needs a path: content → save/follow → email/lead magnet →
consult/booking. One clear CTA per post.

### 8. Wrong-Platform-for-Format
**Tell:** long lectures diced into Reels; polished corporate on TikTok.
**Root cause:** repurposing without respecting each platform's native game.
**Fix:** match format to platform (see platform-playbooks.md). Long-form → YouTube;
myth-busts → Reels/TikTok; POV → LinkedIn/X.

### 9. Vanity-Metric Fixation
**Tell:** judging success by views/likes while ignoring saves, sends, enquiries.
**Root cause:** measuring what's visible, not what matters for the goal.
**Fix:** re-baseline on goal metrics (audit-framework.md). Reframe "success."

### 10. Invisible Positioning
**Tell:** bio is a résumé; no promise; unclear who it's for.
**Root cause:** listing credentials instead of the transformation offered.
**Fix:** bio = who it's for + the outcome + proof + one CTA. Credentials support the promise, they aren't the promise.

### 11. Inconsistent Identity
**Tell:** visual style, tone, and topic differ post to post.
**Root cause:** no content system/brand guide.
**Fix:** lock 3–4 content pillars, a visual template, a consistent voice. Recognisability compounds trust.

### 12. Outsourced Voice with No Soul
**Tell:** agency-produced content that feels generic; the expert's actual
personality and hard-won opinions are absent.
**Root cause:** production company optimised for looking professional, not for
capturing *this person's* unique POV.
**Fix:** WILBA's edge — script from the expert's real opinions/stories via
structured interview, then produce. Authority lives in the take, not the lighting.
