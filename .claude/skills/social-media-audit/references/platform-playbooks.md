# Platform Playbooks (2026)

How each platform actually ranks content, and the authority play for each.
The universal truth first: **every feed platform optimises for retention and
meaningful response, not production value.** Gloss buys nothing the algorithm
cares about. Verify current mechanics with a quick search when it matters —
platforms change ranking signals often.

---

## Instagram (Reels-first)
**How it ranks:** watch-through and re-watches, then sends/shares, saves,
comments, likes — roughly in that order. New content is tested on a small
non-follower pool; strong early retention expands the pool. Reels reach
non-followers; Stories serve existing followers (nurture, not reach).
**Authority play:**
- Hook in frame 1 with an on-screen text promise ("The hormone mistake that ages you fastest").
- 15–40s sweet spot for education; one idea per Reel.
- Talking-head + captions (85% watch muted). Face + text beats b-roll for trust.
- Carousels are the underrated authority format: high saves, long dwell, screenshot-able.
- CTA to *save/send*, not "link in bio" (that suppresses reach). Convert via Stories + DM.
**Failure tell:** cinematic 60s brand films with a 3s logo intro. Dead on arrival.

## YouTube (two engines in one)
**How it ranks:** Long-form is a *search + suggested* engine — CTR × average
view duration drives it; content compounds for years. Shorts is a swipe-feed
retention engine like Reels. They are different games; don't run one strategy.
**Authority play:**
- Long-form = the authority moat. "The truth about bioidentical hormones" style
  titles, 8–15 min, answering the exact questions the ICP googles at 2am.
- Thumbnail + title is 50% of the job. Face + 3–5 word promise.
- Shorts = top-of-funnel discovery that feeds subscribers to long-form.
- This is where produced budget *does* pay off — evergreen search demand.
**Failure tell:** uploading finished ad-style videos with no searchable title/hook.

## TikTok
**How it ranks:** pure retention + rewatch + share; near-zero follower
advantage, so reach is high but loyalty is low. Fastest place to test hooks.
**Authority play:**
- Native, lo-fi, fast. Trends as a delivery vehicle for real expertise.
- "Green-screen over a scary headline and debunk it" is elite for doctors.
- Best used as a *hook laboratory* — find what lands, port winners to Reels/Shorts.
**Failure tell:** polished, slow, corporate. TikTok punishes it hardest of all.

## LinkedIn
**How it ranks:** early engagement from your network + dwell time on the post;
comments weigh heavily; native text/document posts outperform outbound links.
Best organic reach-per-follower of any platform for B2B/professional authority.
**Authority play:**
- For a doctor building professional + referral authority: text posts + carousels
  ("documents"), clinical POV, patient-outcome stories (anonymised, compliant).
- Reaches referrers, journalists, corporate wellness buyers — high-value, low-volume.
**Failure tell:** cross-posting Reels with hashtags. Reads as spam.

## X / Twitter
**How it ranks:** replies + reposts + dwell; rewards frequency and takes.
**Authority play:** real-time commentary, myth-busting threads, links to
long-form. Good for press/peer visibility, weak for consumer lead-gen.

## Content–platform fit matrix (authority client)
| Asset | Best home | Repurpose to |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 8–15 min deep-dive | YouTube long-form | Blog/email, chapters → Shorts/Reels |
| 30s myth-bust | Reels + TikTok + Shorts | LinkedIn video |
| Carousel teaching | Instagram + LinkedIn | PDF lead magnet |
| Q&A / hot take | X + LinkedIn text | Reel with caption |
| Patient-outcome story | LinkedIn + email | Reel (compliant, consented) |

## Compliance note (health/regulated niches)
Medical content must avoid unverified claims, before/afters that breach
platform/ASA/GMC rules, and testimonials where prohibited. Educational framing
("here's how X works") is both safer and higher-authority than promotional
framing. Flag this in every health-client proposal — it protects WILBA and the client.
