# wilba free ai content audit — lead magnet & website funnel

---

## the concept

the wilba.ai website drives every visitor toward one action: **book a free ai content audit.**

this isn't a generic "book a call" — it's a structured, valuable audit that shows coaches and experts exactly where they're leaving content (and money) on the table. it positions you as the expert before they ever pay a cent.

the audit framework below is adapted from the AAA 3-step audit model, tailored specifically for content-focused service businesses (coaches, consultants, course creators, personal brands).

---

## how it works on the website

**visitor journey:**
1. lands on wilba.ai → sees the value prop (ai-powered content that looks like you)
2. every section drives toward one CTA: **"book your free content audit"**
3. clicks CTA → goes to a short intake form (not just a calendar link — you want info first)
4. fills out intake form (5-7 questions, takes 2 minutes)
5. books a 30-minute audit call via calendly
6. you run the audit on the call using the framework below
7. at the end of the call, you present your recommendation + the right wilba package

## the intake form (before the call)

this goes on a dedicated page on wilba.ai — or embedded via typeform/tally. keep it short, warm, and lowercase.

**page headline:** "let's find your content gaps — free."
**subhead:** "answer a few quick questions and we'll show you exactly where ai can multiply your content output without losing your voice."

### questions:

1. **what's your name?** (text field)

2. **what's your website or main social profile?** (text field — lets you research them before the call)

3. **what best describes you?**
   - coach / consultant
   - course creator
   - personal brand / thought leader
   - agency or service business
   - other

4. **what platforms are you currently posting on?** (multi-select)
   - instagram
   - youtube
   - linkedin
   - tiktok
   - podcast
   - email/newsletter
   - none yet

5. **how often are you posting content right now?**
   - daily
   - a few times a week
   - once a week
   - barely / inconsistently
   - not at all

6. **what's your biggest content frustration?** (multi-select)
   - i don't have time to create content
   - i don't know what to say
   - i'm inconsistent — i start and stop
   - my content doesn't convert to clients
   - i hate being on camera
   - i want to be everywhere but can't keep up

7. **what would change for your business if content wasn't a problem anymore?** (open text — this is gold for the sales call)

## the free audit call — 30-minute framework

this is your sales call disguised as a value-packed audit. you're not pitching — you're diagnosing. by the end, the prospect sells themselves.

### structure (30 minutes total)

**minutes 0-5: warm up & context (5 min)**
- thank them for booking
- reference their intake form answers (shows you've done your homework)
- "i've had a look at your [instagram/website/etc.] — let me share what i noticed"

**minutes 5-15: the content audit (10 min)**

this is where you deliver real value. before the call, you've already looked at their online presence. now you walk them through what you found using these 5 audit lenses:

#### 1. visibility score
- how often are they posting? on which platforms?
- are they showing up consistently or in bursts?
- **what you say:** "you've got great stuff, but you're posting [X] times a week. your ideal clients are scrolling past 300 pieces of content a day. they need to see you 3-5x more often to remember you exist."

#### 2. content leverage
- are they repurposing? does a podcast become clips, does a long post become stories?
- **what you say:** "right now, every piece of content you create lives and dies on one platform. you're doing the work once and getting one result. we should be turning every idea into 5-10 pieces across multiple channels."

#### 3. voice & authority
- does their content position them as the expert?
- is it educational, story-driven, or just motivational fluff?
- **what you say:** "your expertise is clear when you talk about [X topic]. the issue is the content doesn't consistently showcase that depth. it's a bit scattered."

#### 4. conversion path
- does their content lead anywhere? is there a CTA? a link? a funnel?
- **what you say:** "i can see you're creating content, but there's no clear path from 'this is helpful' to 'i want to work with you.' every piece of content should be quietly moving people closer to booking."

#### 5. time & effort audit
- how many hours per week are they spending on content?
- what's their process? (winging it vs. batching vs. outsourcing)
- **what you say:** "you're spending [X] hours a week on content. with the right system, we can get you 3-5x the output in a fraction of the time — and it still sounds exactly like you."

**minutes 15-22: the gap & the opportunity (7 min)**

now you connect the dots:
- "here's where you are right now: [summary of audit findings]"
- "here's where you could be: [paint the picture — consistent, multi-platform, converting, automated]"
- "the gap between those two things isn't talent or ideas — you have both. it's a system."

this is where you introduce the wilba approach:
- "what we do is build that system for you. we take your expertise, your voice, your face — and we use AI to turn it into a content machine that runs without you having to sit in front of a camera for hours every week."
- briefly explain the pipeline: research → script → voice clone → avatar video → scheduling

**minutes 22-28: the recommendation (6 min)**

based on what you've found, recommend the right wilba package:
- **strategy & script** — for people who can film but need the ideas and scripts
- **script & avatar video** — for people who want video content without filming
- **full production & scheduling** — for people who want the whole thing done

frame it as: "based on what we've talked about, here's what i'd recommend for you specifically..."

give them a clear next step: "if this feels right, we can get started [this week / next week]. i'll send you the details after this call."

**minutes 28-30: close (2 min)**
- "any questions?"
- "i'll send you a summary of today's audit plus the package details via email"
- "no pressure at all — have a read, and if it resonates, let's go."
