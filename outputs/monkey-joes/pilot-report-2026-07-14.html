The story so far

In roughly eleven weeks of live advertising, the pilot has driven 92 tracked in-store redemptions, built a 258-person opted-in audience inside a ~50,000-contact database, and stood up a full email/SMS engine — the 4th-of-July offer alone reached 253 families. Google credits covered $1,000 of the $4,021 spend, so real out-of-pocket sits near $3,021. Parties haven't been advertised yet; that's the next lever (§6), and the voice agent has already fielded 25 party enquiries.

06

Next lever — parties

Michael's priority is party bookings beating last year. Because "birthday parties Orlando" costs $25–40 per click on Google, paid search is out. The efficient play is a $25-off (BDAY25) offer retargeted on Facebook/Instagram to the warm audience — plus a post-visit "book a party" nudge on email & SMS. Every party is tracked via the code, and the voice agent's 25 party enquiries are ready to convert now.

📋 To close the ROI loop for corporate: confirm BDAY25 is live in Aluvii, and pull the Aluvii party-booking count — the one number that lives outside GHL — so spend can be shown all the way through to booked parties vs last year.
