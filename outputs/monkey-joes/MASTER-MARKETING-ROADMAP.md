## LEVER 5: GOOGLE REVIEWS CAMPAIGN 🌟

**This is the biggest missed opportunity in the whole strategy.**

Google Reviews directly impact:
1. Local search ranking ("kids indoor play near me" → venues with more + higher reviews rank higher)
2. Purchase decisions (82% of consumers read reviews before visiting a local business)
3. Facebook ad trust (potential customers check reviews before clicking)
4. Birthday party conversions (parents booking a $300–$800 party will read every review)

### The Review Goal

**Target:** 4.8+ stars, 200+ reviews per location within 90 days

### Current State Assessment

Check both locations on Google Maps:
- [Pointe Orlando current review count + rating]
- [Winter Park current review count + rating]
- [Competitors' review counts for comparison]

### The Review Engine (3 channels)

**Channel 1: Email automation (Sequence F above)**
- Trigger: 48 hours post-visit
- Ask Michael to personalise the sending — "from Michael" with real email, not a generic address
- Estimated uplift: 5–10 reviews/month

**Channel 2: In-venue QR code signage**
- Place at exit, party room checkout desk, seating area
- Print simple A5 card: "Had a great time? Tell other families. [QR code → Google Review page]"
- Script for staff: "If you had a great time today — it would mean a lot to us if you left us a quick Google review. Here's the QR code."
- Estimated uplift: 10–20 reviews/month

**Channel 3: Birthday party check-out ask**
- Staff ask at the end of every party: "Did the kids have a great time? We'd love a quick review — we'll pop a QR code in the party bag."
- Birthday parents are your warmest possible reviewer — they just had a 2-hour positive experience
- Estimated uplift: 5–10 reviews/month per party booked

**Channel 4: Dormant database re-engagement**
- Send one email to the 36K waiver database (non-active): "Hi — we'd love to know how we did. If you've visited us before, would you leave us a Google review? It helps other families find us."
- One-time blast. Even a 0.5% response rate = 180 reviews
- This alone could transform both Google listings within weeks

### Responding to Reviews (Michael's role)

Every review — positive or negative — needs a response within 48 hours.

**Positive review response template:**
> "Thank you so much [Name]! Knowing [your child / the kids] had such a great time makes every Wacky Wednesday worth it. We're so glad you got to breathe for a bit too 😄 See you again soon! — Michael"

**Negative review response template:**
> "Hi [Name], thank you for letting us know — this genuinely isn't the experience we work hard to create. Please reach out directly to [email/phone] so we can make it right. — Michael, Owner"

*Never argue with a negative review. Respond publicly, resolve privately.*

### Removing Fake/Outdated Negative Reviews

If there are old negative reviews that are:
- From competitors (common in this category)
- Factually incorrect
- Violate Google policy (spam, off-topic, personal attacks)

Flag them for Google removal via the Google Business Profile dashboard. This is often overlooked — a simple cleanup can move a 4.2 to a 4.5 just by removing illegitimate reviews.

## LEVER 6: WALK-IN BOOST TACTICS

### Goal: Increase spontaneous/unplanned visits by 20%+ within 60 days

Walk-ins are the highest-margin visits — no ad cost, no booking friction. These tactics cost nothing or near-nothing.

---

### Tactic 1: Google Business Profile Optimisation

Your Google listing is your most valuable free marketing asset. Most competitors ignore it after setup.

**Weekly tasks:**
- Post a Google Business update every Tuesday ("Wacky Wednesday tomorrow — BOGO!") and every Thursday ("Toddler Thursday today — come bounce!")
- These posts appear in Google Maps and local search results — completely free
- Use photos with actual kids (real, genuine smiles — not stock)
- Add "BOGO Wednesday" as a Google Business Offer post (free feature)
- Ensure both locations have: correct hours, correct phone number, correct address, Q&A answered, all photos uploaded and tagged

**One-time setup:**
- Add "BOGO Wednesdays" and "Toddler Thursdays" to the business description
- List all amenities: AC, seating for parents, party rooms, wheelchair accessible, free parking
- Add "Popular Times" note in description (helpful: "Quieter on weekday mornings — great for toddlers")

**Impact:** Businesses that post weekly on Google Business see 2× the views vs those that don't.

## LEVER 10: REFERRAL PROGRAM

### How It Works

Simple. No app. No points system.

1. After 3rd visit (Jumper Card milestone), automatic email goes out (Sequence G)
2. Parent gets a unique code: `REFER-[THEIRNAME]`
3. They share it with a friend
4. Friend gets first BOGO admission free
5. When friend uses it, original parent gets one free admission added to their Jumper Card

**Economic math:**
- You give away 1 free admission (cost = admission price)
- You acquire a new customer (lifetime value = multiple visits + potential birthday party)
- Net: massive positive ROI

### Promoting the Referral Program

- Email Sequence G (automated after 3rd visit)
- In-venue sign: "Love it here? Bring a friend — you both get a free bounce."
- Mention in every birthday party follow-up email
