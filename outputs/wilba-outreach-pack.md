## 3. The follow-up (send 2-3 days after, if no reply)
> Hey [name] — no worries if now's not the time! Just didn't want you to miss it. The audit's free and takes 3 min, and honestly the number surprises most people. Want me to send it through? 🙂

## 4. The referral ask (after any good audit / chat)
> Really glad that was useful [name]! Quick favour — know anyone else who'd want their number? Happy to run a free audit for them too. Even one intro would mean a lot. 🙏

## 5. The audit-done follow-up (→ book the call)
> Hi [name], had a look at your audit answers and wanted to reach out personally — the number's a bit confronting. Based on what you told me, you're looking at roughly $[X] a month going to voicemail. Full breakdown's attached. If you want, I'll walk you through what we'd fix first on a quick 15-min call — no pitch. Here's my link: [Calendly]

---

## 6. Objection quick-responses
- **"Is it expensive?"** → "The audit's free, and most clients recover more in the first month than the setup costs. I'll show you the numbers before you decide anything."
- **"Will it sound robotic?"** → "Fair worry — and no. You set the voice and words. I can literally call mine so you hear it. Way warmer than voicemail, which is what you've got now."
- **"I'm not techy."** → "Perfect — neither am I. That's the whole point. You describe it, we build it, you don't touch the tech."
- **"I'll think about it."** → "Totally. Want me to just run the free audit so you've got the actual number while you think? No commitment."
