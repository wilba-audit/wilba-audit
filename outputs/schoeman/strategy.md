Every post belongs to one pillar. This makes her recognisable and the algorithm confident about who to show her to.

1. **"You're not imagining it"** — validating dismissed symptoms (the emotional hook; her single strongest angle vs. Sarah's wound). *Highest save/share potential.*

5. **⭐ The Schoeman method (signature)** — her POV, why functional, consented/anonymised patient turnarounds, "a day in my clinic," honest takes on industry nonsense. *This is where authority + trust are built. Nobody can copy her voice.*
6. **Men's hormones (secondary)** — andropause, testosterone honesty. Small cadence, underserved, low competition.

## 7. The hook system + a starter hook bank

The audit's #1 fix. First 2 seconds + on-screen text promise decide reach. Build/test 3 hooks per idea. Winners get reused.

**Hook formulas (with her-niche examples):**
- *Callout:* "If you're 45+ and waking at 3am, this is for you."
- *Myth-bust:* "No, HRT does not 'just delay the inevitable.' Here's the truth."
- *Contrarian:* "Your 'normal' blood test is hiding the problem. Let me show you."
- *Cost of inaction:* "Ignoring perimenopause for 5 years costs you more than sleep."
- *Story open:* "A patient came in convinced she had early dementia. It was her hormones."
- *Number:* "3 signs your fatigue is hormonal, not 'just life'."
- *Permission:* "You're not lazy. You're not crazy. You're perimenopausal."
- *Question they're googling:* "Is bioidentical HRT safe? A doctor's honest answer."
- *Us-vs-them:* "What the supplement influencers won't tell you about menopause."
- *Direct promise:* "The hormone work-up your GP didn't do — explained in 60 seconds."

**Rule:** never open with a logo, "Hi I'm Dr Schoeman," or a slow set-up. Payoff first, intro never (or last).

## 8. Own the audience: lead magnet + email funnel

Reach is rented; an email list is owned. This is the audit's "fuel line" fix and the difference between fleeting views and a real business asset.

- **Lead magnet:** *"Is It Your Hormones? The 27-symptom checklist your GP won't run"* (or *"The Perimenopause Blood-Test Guide"*). High-intent, name-and-email gated, links from every bio + pinned content.
- **Nurture sequence (5 emails):** (1) welcome + the checklist, (2) "why 'normal' bloods mislead," (3) BHRT honestly, (4) a patient turnaround story, (5) how a Schoeman consult works + booking.
- **Weekly email:** repurpose the week's best content; the one channel no algorithm can throttle.

## 13. Compliance guardrails (protect her credibility & WILBA)

Her trust is the asset; over-claiming destroys it (and breaches rules):
- **Educational framing > promotional.** "Here's how X works," not "X will cure you."
- **No unverified claims;** cite evidence; acknowledge nuance (this *is* her differentiator vs. hype-sellers).
- **Testimonials/patient stories:** only where GMC/ASA-permitted, anonymised, consented.
- **BHRT specifically:** be precise about "bioidentical vs body-identical," safety, and what's licensed vs. compounded. Precision reads as authority here.
