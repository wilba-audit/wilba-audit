## 1. Who she is (the frame)

Dr Gina Schoeman is a functional & longevity medicine doctor with 20+ years'
experience, specialising in **bioidentical hormone therapy** for women
(perimenopause/menopause) and men (andropause). Harley Street + Wimbledon +
telehealth, premium private-pay London market. Stellenbosch MBChB, MBA,
dermatology diploma (Cardiff), MRCGP, IFM + Marion Gluck trained. The Schoeman
Clinic; also affiliated with CREO Clinic.

**This is an A-grade authority niche.** Menopause and longevity are two of the
highest-intent, fastest-growing health conversations of the decade, with a
huge, underserved, ready-to-pay audience of women who feel dismissed by
mainstream care. She has genuine credentials, a clear specialty, and a
premium offer. The raw materials for authority are *all here.* Which is exactly
why "spent money on video, no traction" is frustrating — and fixable.

**The niche is *proven* to work on social — so this is a strategy problem, not a
market problem.** Benchmarks in her exact space: Dr Louise Newson (UK,
@menopause_doctor) ~826K followers `[REAL]`; Dr Mary Claire Haver (US,
@drmaryclaire) ~4M IG / 2.2M TikTok `[REAL]`. Critically, **they won on
relatability, myth-busting and consistency — not production value** — which is
the exact opposite of where Gina's budget went. Newson is Gina's template (UK,
evidence-based, hormone-led, built owned assets); Haver is the cautionary
contrast (huge reach but sells supplements — "raised eyebrows"). Gina's open
lane is *the honest, premium, functional doctor.* See `strategy.md` for the full
play. **YouTube status:** no active channel for the right Dr Schoeman could be
found publicly `[CONFIRM]` — treated as untapped opportunity, not existing asset.

## 2. The ICP she should own
The person the content must reach (to be confirmed with her, but almost
certainly): **a 45–55 year-old professional woman — brain fog, weight gain,
broken sleep, low libido, anxiety — who's been told by her GP that her bloods
are "normal" and to come back in a year.** She is googling her symptoms at 2am,
she has money, and she is desperate for someone who will actually *listen* and
explain. Win her trust and she books, stays for years, and refers her friends.
Everything should be built for her.

## 3. The likely root causes (why the videos aren't travelling)

Based on the pattern that produces "expensive video, no reach," here are the
most probable culprits. Each maps to a known, solvable failure pattern. We'll
confirm exact severity against her analytics, but in this niche these are the
usual suspects — in priority order:

1. **The Production-Value Fallacy** *(the big one)* — Money almost certainly
   went into *how the videos look* (crew, edit, gloss) rather than the two
   things platforms actually reward: the **hook** (first 2 seconds) and
   **retention structure**. A £-heavy film and a phone selfie compete on the
   same metric — watch-through — and polish doesn't win it. `[CONFIRM: average
   watch % on her Reels/YouTube]`

2. **Talking to peers, not to patients** — The expert's curse. Clinically
   accurate, credential-forward content that would impress colleagues but sails
   over the head of the frightened 48-year-old it needs to reach. Authority for
   *her audience* comes from translating, not from precision.

3. **No hook system + wrong-platform-for-format** — If produced videos are
   polished 60-second brand films posted as Reels/TikToks, they're structurally
   doomed there. Long, slow, branded openings are exit ramps. Meanwhile the one
   platform where that production value *does* compound — YouTube long-form
   (evergreen search: "bioidentical hormones explained," "is HRT safe") — may be
   under-used. `[CONFIRM: platform mix + YouTube presence]`

4. **Post-once-and-pray** — Likely little repurposing (one shoot → one post)
   and probably no owned email capture, so every bit of reach is rented and
   nothing compounds. `[CONFIRM: cadence + email list]`

5. **Dead-end conversion path** — Even great content leaks if there's no clear
   next step from "interesting doctor" → "booked consult." `[CONFIRM: link
   clicks, enquiry attribution]`

There is **nothing wrong with her or her expertise.** The spend was aimed at the
wrong target. That's a strategy problem, and strategy is fixable fast.

## 5. Prognosis (honest timeline)
Authority compounds; it does not spike. Realistic path if we fix the foundations:
- **Months 1–3:** Reposition profile to a patient-promise; lock ICP + content
  pillars; install a hook system; establish a sustainable cadence; stand up
  YouTube-as-authority-moat + email capture. *Leading indicators:* watch % up,
  saves/shares up, first quality DMs.
- **Months 3–6:** Consistent qualified reach; saves/shares climb; first inbound
  enquiries attributable to social; a growing owned email list.
- **Months 6–12:** Authority signals compound; branded search rises; inbound
  becomes a reliable, low-cost patient channel that supports the premium offer.

**What we will NOT promise:** a follower number or "going viral." We promise a
system, the leading indicators we control, and monthly truth on what's working.

## 6. The two genuine strengths (build on these)
1. **World-class expertise in a red-hot niche** (dim. 5 = 4/5). The hardest thing
   to manufacture — credibility — she already has.
2. **A premium, high-margin offer with a desperate, underserved audience.** Even
   modest, well-targeted reach converts to real revenue here. Small wins pay.

## 7. Immediate high-leverage moves (preview of the plan)
1. Rewrite the profile from *credentials* → *patient promise* + one clear CTA.
2. Pick ONE ICP; rebuild content pillars around her 2am questions.
3. Install a hook system; re-cut existing footage into hook-first short-form (use
   the spend already made — no reshoot needed to start).
4. Stand up YouTube long-form as the evergreen authority moat.
5. Start an email list + a simple lead magnet ("The Perimenopause Blood-Test
   Guide Your GP Won't Give You") to convert rented reach into owned audience.
6. Repurpose every shoot into 8–12 touches across platforms.
