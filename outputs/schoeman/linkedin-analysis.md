## 2. The three findings that matter (grounded, not inferred)

### ① Her presence is fragmented across duplicate profiles ★ top fix
Two profile exports with **different follower counts (1,513 vs 2,349)** = two
separate LinkedIn identities, plus a dormant company page (12). You also gave a
different profile URL (`…-afmcp`) than the one previously indexed (`…-022a07b`).
**Why it's costing her:** a follower on one never sees the other; the algorithm
splits her signal in half; a patient/journalist Googling her finds two Ginas and
trusts neither fully. **Fix:** pick ONE primary profile, make it definitive,
retire/redirect the other, set the company page to reshare. `[CONFIRM which is which]`

### ② She posts in bursts, not a rhythm
Views swing **30–37×** day to day. That isn't the algorithm being fickle — it's
the fingerprint of **sporadic posting**: a spike on the day she posts (~1,000
views), then collapse to 27–60 on the silent days. Consistency is the highest-
leverage change available, and it costs nothing but a schedule.

### ③ Her content informs but doesn't get *shared* — the authority killer
**Reposts across the whole week: 1 and 0.** On LinkedIn, reach compounds through
reposts and comments — a post that isn't shared dies inside her existing network.
That's exactly what the low, erratic **Reach** figures show (as low as 1–17 on
several days): she's mostly reaching people who already follow her, not breaking
out. **Her content is a broadcast, not a conversation.** This is the ceiling.

## 4. The LinkedIn strategy (grounded in the above)

**Goal metric on LinkedIn = reposts + quality comments from the right people**
(clinicians, press, corporate-wellness buyers, affluent patients) — NOT views.

1. **Consolidate to ONE profile (Week 1).** Choose the primary (recommend the one
   she'll actively post from and that matches her `…-afmcp` URL). Update the other
   to point to it (or wind it down); switch the company page to reshare her posts.
   Kills the fragmentation in one move. `[CONFIRM]`
2. **Fixed cadence: 3 posts/week, same days.** End the 30× volatility. A live
   profile keeps baseline views up even on non-post days. Batch to make it survivable.
3. **Engineer for shares, not just views.** The formats that get reposted here:
   - **Document carousels** (teach one concept — highest save/share on LinkedIn).
   - **Strong-POV text posts** — a clear, brave clinical opinion ("'Normal' bloods
     are failing women. Here's what your GP isn't testing.").
   - **Consented, anonymised patient-outcome stories** — the most shared, most
     trusted format for a doctor (GMC/ASA-compliant).
   - **Myth-busts** on hormones/menopause news — positions her as the interpreter.
4. **Hook the first line.** Only the first ~2 lines show before "…see more." Open
   with tension/promise, never a preamble. (Reuse the hook bank in `strategy.md`.)
5. **Links in the first comment, never the post body** — outbound links in-body
   suppress reach. Drive to the lead magnet from the comments.
6. **15 minutes/day in the comments** — thoughtful replies on peers', journalists'
   and patients' posts. This is how she breaks the network ceiling the data shows.
7. **Track the right thing.** Watch **reposts, comments, and profile→follow**, not
   view spikes. Reposts going from ~0 to 5+/post is the real leading indicator.
