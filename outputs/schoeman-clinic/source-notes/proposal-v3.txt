The Situation

Dr. Gina is a premium-tier longevity and functional-medicine doctor, charging £500 per 
consultation, with an internationally distributed patient base and a clinic she launched only 
seven months ago. The signal is strong, she is already busy enough that she's bringing on a 
second doctor in June, and one new doctor every six months after that.

Operations have not kept pace with that growth. Three remote admin staff work different days 
and share patient WhatsApp messages by forwarding screenshots. Fifty-ﬁve patients are 
overdue for a follow-up, but no one has sent. Patient comms are fragmented across email, 
PDF, WhatsApp, and phone. Payments are slower than they should be. Dr. Gina has new 
products, education, and recall sequences she wants to push to her existing base, and no 
working way to do it at scale.

Currently Semble handles the clinical side cleanly, notes, prescriptions, pathology, the Heidi 
AI scribe, and will keep doing so. What Semble was never built to do is run the operational 
and marketing side of a growing premium practice: a shared inbox for distributed admins, 
automated nurture, recall, payment chasing, newsletters, and an after-hours voice agent. That 
is the gap this build closes.

The marketing agency's website + Google Ads relaunch is targeted for June. Three months 
from now, operational throughput needs to roughly double. Without the systems below in 
place, the gap between what current operations can handle and what the clinic will need to 
handle widens every week.

What We're Proposing

A Phase 1 build that centralises everything operational into GoHighLevel while leaving Dr. 
Gina's clinical workﬂow in Semble, where it belongs. The build covers six features in priority 
order:


● 1. Uniﬁed WhatsApp patient communication - a shared inbox for the three admin staff, 

replacing the current screenshot-and-forward chaos. 

● 2. Recall, nurture, and newsletter automation - the 55-patient backlog cleared, plus 

ongoing 3- and 6-month recall, payment chasing, blood-test chasing, reminders, and 
the broadcast capability Dr. Gina has been missing. 

● 3. After-hours voice agent - operational queries only, with a hard escalation rule for 

anything medical.

What Stays in Semble, What Moves to 
GoHighLevel

One point to state up front: clinical records stay in Semble. They are not migrating to 
GoHighLevel.

This is an architectural constraint, not a preference. GoHighLevel is a marketing CRM with a 
HIPAA-tier add-on; it was never built as a clinical record system. 

Custom objects cap at 10 ﬁelds; no UK is e-prescribing with Advanced Electronic Signature 
(legally required for UK private scripts); no pathology or pharmacy network; no Heidi AI scribe 
integration; no clinical-grade audit trail CQC inspectors can search against; and GoHighLevel 
hosts customer data on US servers with no UK/EEA residency option. 

Using GoHighLevel as Dr. Gina's clinical record system would breach CQC Regulation 17 
from day one and collapse her existing Heidi transcription workﬂow, which is her actual day-
to-day clinical productivity tool.

What moves to GoHighLevel is everything operational: the three-admin WhatsApp inbox, the 
nurture/recall/newsletter engine, forms, payment ﬂow, patient journey automation, the voice 
agent. Semble and GoHighLevel talk to each other via webhook. 

The two systems do different jobs and stay out of each other's way. 

Phase 1 — Detailed Scope 
Feature 1 — Uniﬁed WhatsApp Patient Communication 

● Shared inbox: Shared inbox for three remote admin staff on GoHighLevel's HIPAA-
tier Conversations module. All three see the same patient thread in real time, assign 
conversations to each other, leave internal notes invisible to the patient, and pick up 
from one another across different working days. Native — no add-ons. 

● WhatsApp channel: WhatsApp Business channel (+44 7426 494321, already set up) 

migrated from the consumer WhatsApp Business app to WhatsApp Business API via 
Meta Cloud API with Local Storage Region set to GB. Content stays on UK servers. 

● Outbound automation: Automated outbound for: post-consultation advice, payment 
links (Stripe), registration and onboarding forms, booking links for consultation calls. 

● Post-consult summary: Composed from Semble consultation data, prescription 

details, pharmacy, supply length, follow-up blood test requirements, next steps, sent 
within minutes of Dr. Gina closing the consultation.

● Inbound FAQ handling: Common operational questions (blood test prep, fasting 

rules, address, parking) answered from a scripted response library approved by Dr. 
Gina, not freely generated. Anything clinical or outside the approved set routes directly 
to an admin, not the AI.

● Audit logging: All inbound/outbound messages logged for audit under the clinic's

Feature 2 — Recall, Nurture, and Newsletter Automation 

● 55-patient overdue backlog: One-time recall in the clinic's voice inviting each 

overdue patient to rebook. Drafted by us, approved by Dr. Gina, sent in a single batch. 

● 3-month and 6-month recall: Dr. Gina tags the interval at consultation; the tag drives 
the schedule. Reminders are personalised based on the original consultation context. 

● Pre-appointment reminders: 48h + 24h nudges with intake-form and blood-test 

completion checks. No-shows trigger a separate ﬂow. 

● Payment and blood-test chase sequences: For enquiries that haven't paid or 
haven't completed the blood test required before the consultation can go ahead. 

● Newsletter and broadcast capability: Dr. Gina sends new-product announcements, 
patient education, and clinic updates to her patient base from a single compose view. 
Segmentable by patient tag, consent status, or custom ﬁeld. 

● Onboarding ﬂow: New patient says "register" → automated link to the long intake 
form → reminder sequence if incomplete → blood test order → payment link → 
consultation booking handoff. 

Feature 3 — After-Hours Voice Agent 

● Number setup: Twilio UK number. Dr. Gina's existing line (+44 20 3006 3224) stays 

published; calls divert to the AI number on no-answer outside business hours. 

● Operational scope: Natural-voice AI handles operational queries: appointment times 

for existing bookings, pharmacy locations, prescription pickup info, clinic hours, 
directions. Knowledge base assembled from the website and 2–3 PDFs Dr. Gina 
supplies. 

● What it does NOT do: New booking requests are routed to the registration form or to 

an admin for callback. The agent does not book new appointments in Phase 1. 

● Hard escalation rule: The agent does not offer medical advice or triage. Anything 
resembling a medical question triggers one of two scripted responses: "if this is 
urgent, hang up and call 999" or "I'll have someone from the team call you back 
tomorrow between 9 and 5." Call is logged with a callback request to Lisa. 

● AI disclosure: Opening script includes a short AI disclosure required by ICO and 

CQC guidance. 

● Cost displacement: Replaces the after-hours portion of Moneypenny (~£190/month). 
Daytime Moneypenny can stay for overﬂow or wind down entirely — Dr. Gina's call. 

Feature 4 — Strategic Patient Journey Redesign 
This is the piece that's been missing from the conversation so far, and it's the highest-
leverage piece in the build. 

The current journey forces every prospective patient to ﬁll out a 20-ﬁeld intake form before 
they've committed any money — including ID upload, full medical history, allergies, and 
consent. People drop out before they ever see a calendar. 

The redesigned journey is paid ﬁrst, qualiﬁed second:

● Step 1: Short interim inquiry page (built on existing WordPress so it's live in Week 2, 

not waiting on the agency's June redesign). 6 ﬁelds max: name, email, phone, gender, 
primary concern (drop-down only — no free text), referral source. 

● Step 2: Inquiry hits GoHighLevel as a lead. Auto-conﬁrmation email + WhatsApp sent 

within 60 seconds. 

● Step 3: Long intake form sent on a separate link. Reminders every 2 days if 

incomplete. 

● Step 4: Once intake is complete, automated blood-test order, home kit (most patients) 

or London clinic (UK-based, prefer in-person) and the payment link for the test. 

● Step 5: Once blood test is paid + booked, consultation calendar opens. Patient pays 

£500 and books. 

● Step 6: Post-consultation: summary message → prescription pickup info → 3- or 6-

month recall tag set by Dr. Gina at consultation → recall ﬁres automatically when the 
time comes.

This is the entire Schoeman Clinic patient lifecycle on one rail, and it solves the dropout 
problem the current 20-ﬁeld form is causing right now. 

Feature 5 — Dr. Gina's Daily-Brief Agent (Bonus) 
Originally scoped for Phase 2, moved into Phase 1 at no extra cost. A short morning brief 
delivered to Dr. Gina's WhatsApp at 7am UK time: 

● Today's conﬁrmed appointments (count + ﬁrst/last) 

● New enquiries from the last 24 hours 

● Overdue items (intake forms, blood tests, payments) 

● Anything human-in-the-loop is sitting in the admin queue 

● Yesterday's revenue / this-week-to-date 

She wakes up already knowing where the clinic stands. No more chasing the PA to ask "have 
you called X back yet?" — the brief tells her. 

Feature 6 — Replicable Doctor-Onboarding Stack 
Every workﬂow, template, scripted response, voice-agent prompt, and form is built as a 
reusable template with documented variables. When Dr. Gina onboards her second doctor in 
June, we duplicate the template, swap a handful of variables (name, calendar, fee, intake 
nuances), and the new doctor goes live. No new build engagement required. 

This is the structural difference between buying an automation and buying an automation 
platform. At one new doctor every six months, this saves the clinic somewhere between 
£15K–£40K in build fees over the next two years, included in this engagement at no additional 
cost. 

WordPress Inquiry Page (Interim) 
A short form on the existing website so the June Google Ads have somewhere to land before 
the marketing agency's redesign ships. Our team handles the WordPress side. Designed to 
be drop-in-replaceable when the agency's new site goes live.
