1 What's changing

All patient WhatsApp messages now come into one shared inbox in GoHighLevel (GHL), in the Conversations tab. Everyone works from the same place, in real time. No more one person holding the phone, no more forwarding screenshots between you.

2 Finding your messages

Open the Conversations tab — that's where you'll live.

  • Left — the list of conversations, newest and unread at the top.
  • Middle — the message thread you're reading.
  • Right — the patient's details and history.
  • Filters at the top: Unread Assigned to me All — spend your time in Unread and Assigned to me.

3 Replying to a patient

  1. Click the conversation to open it.
  2. Type your reply in the box at the bottom.
  3. Press Send — it goes straight to the patient on WhatsApp.
If your message won't send

WhatsApp only lets us free-type for 24 hours after the patient's last message. If it's been longer, your reply may be blocked — you'll need to send an approved template message to reopen the conversation. That's normal, not a fault.

4 Leaving internal notes

You can leave a note that only the team sees — the patient never does. Use it to say what you've done or what's needed: chased Dr Gina, waiting on payment, escalated a clinical question.

The golden rule

✓Before you send, always check: am I replying to the patient, or leaving a note for my team? A reply and a note look different on screen — take the half-second to be sure.

5 The best way to write a note

A few simple habits keep notes useful instead of noisy.

  • Start with a status tag so anyone can scan the thread at a glance:
ESCALATED WAITING ON PAYMENT BLOODS BOOKED CHASING DONE
  • @mention a teammate when you're handing it over, so they know it's theirs.
  • Keep it short and factual — GHL already records who wrote it and when, so you don't need to sign it.
  • Always leave a note before you close a conversation, so the next person has the full picture.
Example note

ESCALATED — patient asked about blood results. Emailed Dr Gina + saved to Semble file. @Zoey to follow up if she books.

6 Working as a team

  • Assign a conversation to yourself when you pick it up — so two people don't reply to the same patient.
  • Mark it unread to hand it back to the team.
  • The rhythm: assign to yourself → note what you did → mark unread if it needs someone else.

7 Keeping your calendar current

WILBA sets up the clinic calendars — you don't need to build or configure anything. Each of you is linked in with your own connected calendar, and when a patient books they simply get whoever's free.

Your one job

Keep your own linked calendar up to date — connect it, and block out any time off or when you're unavailable. As long as it's current, your availability updates automatically. Nothing to manage by hand.

8 When to escalate

Never answer anything clinical

No results, no medication or dosing, no symptoms, no "is this safe". These always go to a human.

  • To escalate: copy the message to Dr Gina by email and paste it onto the patient's Semble file, then leave an internal note (tag it ESCALATED).
  • Blood results are discussed at an appointment — never sent over WhatsApp.
  • The 2-email rule: a patient gets two short email replies; anything longer or complex becomes a paid consultation.

9 What's not changing

  • Appointments and follow-ups are still booked in Semble, exactly as now — and Semble still sends the reminders.
  • AI-suggested replies and the knowledge base are coming later, with their own training. For now, every reply is written by you.
