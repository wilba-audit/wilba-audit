Running the session

  1. Frame it2 min

    "From today, every patient WhatsApp lands in one shared inbox inside GoHighLevel. No more forwarding screenshots or one person holding the phone. You all see the same conversation in real time and can hand off to each other across your different days."

  2. Tour the Conversations tab5 min

    • Left = the conversation list (unread at top). Middle = the thread. Right = the patient's details.
    • Filters at the top: Unread / Assigned to me / All — live in Unread and Assigned to me.
    • The channel icon shows WhatsApp vs SMS/email — every channel, one place.

  3. Reply to a patient8 min

    • Open a conversation → type in the box at the bottom → Send.
    The 24-hour window — the #1 "why won't it send?" You can free-text a patient for 24 hours after their last message. After that, WhatsApp only allows an approved template — the box may block you. If a normal reply won't send, that's why: send a template to reconnect. (Confirm template names with Griffin.)

  4. Internal notes — the safety lesson6 min

    Slow down here — highest-risk thing in the room
    • Leave a note the team sees but the patient never does — "chased Gina re bloods", "waiting on payment", "clinical, escalating".
    • A reply-to-patient looks different from an internal note (colour / label / tab). Every time, ask: "Am I talking to the patient, or my team?"
    • Demo both ways live — show a note stay internal and a reply go out.

  5. Work as a team6 min

    • Assign a conversation to yourself when you pick it up, so two PAs don't double-reply.
    • Mark unread to leave it for someone else; @mention a teammate in a note if available.
    • Agree the norm out loud: assign to yourself → internal-note what you did → mark unread if it needs another pair of hands.

  6. Calendars — the 60-second version4 min

    Keep this high-level. You are not teaching them to build or configure calendars — WILBA sets those up. They just need to understand the one thing that's on them.

    • WILBA builds the clinic calendar (e.g. discovery calls) with the clinic's set hours. The team doesn't configure that.
    • Each of you is linked into it with your own connected calendar. When a patient books, they don't pick a person — the system offers whoever's free.
    • Your only job: keep your linked calendar up to date — connect it, and block out time off / anything you're unavailable for.
    The one takeaway As long as your linked calendar is up to date, availability stays accurate on its own — there's nothing to manage by hand. If you're ever off, block it in your calendar and the booking slots update automatically.

  7. Escalation basics5 min

    • Never answer anything clinical — no results, dosing, symptoms, or "is this safe". Do not answer.
    • Keep the current process: copy the message to Dr Gina by email and paste it onto the patient's Semble file, then leave an internal note in GHL.
    • Blood results are discussed at an appointment — never sent over WhatsApp.
    • Gina's rule: 2 short emails max, then it becomes a paid consult.

  8. What is NOT changing tonight4 min

    • Consultations & follow-ups are still booked in Semble, exactly as now — and Semble still sends the appointment reminders. GHL does nothing on booking tonight.
    • The GHL discovery-call calendar and AI-suggested replies come later, each with its own walkthrough.

  9. Wrap2 min

    • "This is deliberately simple for now. As you get comfortable we'll add AI-suggested replies — a PA still approves every one — and the knowledge base. Not today."
    • Tell them a written recap follows, and they can WhatsApp you questions.
