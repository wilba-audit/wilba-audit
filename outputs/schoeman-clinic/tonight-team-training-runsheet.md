## The session (≈35–40 min)

### 1. Frame it (2 min)
"From today, every patient WhatsApp lands in one shared inbox inside GoHighLevel. No more forwarding screenshots or one person holding the phone. You all see the same conversation, in real time, and you can hand off to each other across your different days."

### 2. The tour — the Conversations tab (5 min)
- **Left = the list** of conversations (newest/unread at top). **Middle = the thread.** **Right = the patient's details** (name, phone, tags, history).
- **Filters** at the top: *Unread*, *Assigned to me*, *All*. Teach them to live in **Unread** and **Assigned to me**.
- The channel icon shows it's WhatsApp (vs SMS/email) — everything's in the same place.

### 3. Replying to a patient (8 min) — the core skill
- Click a conversation → type in the **message box at the bottom** → **Send**. That's it.
- **⚠️ The one rule that trips people up (WhatsApp's 24-hour window):** you can freely reply for **24 hours after the patient's last message**. If it's been **longer than 24h**, WhatsApp only allows a pre-approved **template** message, not free text — the box may block you or require picking a template. Tell them: *"If your normal reply won't send, it's because the 24-hour window closed — use an approved template, or send a quick 'reconnecting' template to reopen it."* (Confirm exact template names with Griffin.)
- Show **emoji/attachments** if they use them.

### 4. Internal notes — the safety lesson (6 min) 🔒
**This is the highest-risk thing to get wrong, so slow down here.**
- There's a way to leave a note **for the team only** — the patient never sees it. Use it for "chased Dr Gina re: bloods," "waiting on payment," "this one's clinical, escalating."
- **The visual difference:** a reply-to-patient looks different from an internal note (different colour/label/tab). **Teach them to check every time before they hit send:** *"Am I talking to the patient, or to my team?"*
- Do a **live demo both ways** so they see a note stay internal and a reply go out.
- *(If the exact control isn't obvious in your sub-account, have Griffin confirm the button name before the call — don't guess live.)*

### 5. Working as a team (6 min)
- **Assign** a conversation to a person (so two PAs don't reply to the same patient). Teach: *"If you pick it up, assign it to yourself."*
- **Mark read / unread** — leave it unread if you want someone else to action it.
- **@mention / tag** a teammate in a note if GHL supports it in this account.
- Agree a simple team norm out loud: *"Assign to yourself when you start; internal-note what you did; mark unread if it needs another pair of hands."*

### 6. The escalation basics (5 min) — this matters at a clinic
- **Anything clinical → do NOT answer.** No results, no dosing, no symptoms, no "is this safe."
- Current process (keep doing it): copy the message to **Dr Gina by email** AND paste it onto the patient's **Semble file**, then leave an **internal note** in GHL so the team knows it's been escalated.
- Blood results are **discussed at an appointment** — never sent over WhatsApp.
- Reminder of Gina's rule: **2 short emails max**, then it's a paid consult.

### 7. Wrap (2 min)
- "This is deliberately simple for now. As you get comfortable, we'll layer in AI-suggested replies (a PA still approves every one) and the knowledge base — but not today."
- Tell them a **Loom walkthrough** will follow so they can rewatch, and they can WhatsApp you questions.
