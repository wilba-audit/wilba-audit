3 Clinical safety and escalation

The framework is agreed. Every message sits at one of three levels. These are the last points to make it watertight.

Operational
Handled by the team: bookings, rescheduling, blood logistics, pricing, locations, pharmacy status, admin.
Clinical
Passed to a clinician, never answered by the team or assistant: symptoms, results, medication, "is this safe", or a specific patient's history.
Urgent
Immediate human and a priority alert: a mental health crisis, self harm, or a medical emergency.
  • Two or three examples of questions that sound operational but are actually clinical.
  • The exact holding message for a medication question ("should I change my dose", "is this side effect normal") while a clinician takes over.
  • Urgent and safeguarding: what the patient is told immediately, which words must always trigger a priority alert, and who is notified and how.
  • An overdue patient requesting a repeat prescription: what should the team do?
  • The short list of anything the assistant must never answer.

Part two — Digital presence

5 Social media & brand: the audit

A look at how the clinic shows up online, so the foundations are right before anyone invests in marketing.

How we looked at it

Five lenses: discoverability (can a searching patient find you), credibility (does the presence match a premium, CQC-registered clinic), content (what is posted, and for whom), conversion (does interest lead to an enquiry), and foundations (healthy accounts and consistent branding).

Strengths to build on

  • Dr Gina's authority and credentials, and a clear, in-demand niche in hormone, longevity and functional medicine.
  • Real patient outcomes and testimonials.
  • Dr Gina comes across naturally and warmly on camera.
  • A genuinely premium, CQC-registered positioning.

Opportunities

  • The online presence does not yet match the quality of the clinic itself.
  • Discoverability: making sure a searching patient easily finds you (website and Google Business Profile).
  • The website, mid-transition, is the front door and the priority to get right.
  • A clear, obvious path from social and site to a discovery call or enquiry.

Recommendation: foundations first

  • Strengthen the fundamentals first: consistent branding, a working website, discoverability, and a simple, authoritative content rhythm built around Dr Gina's expertise, rather than chasing followers or paid ads.
  • Scale marketing once the clinic has capacity (the new doctors bedding in) and the path from interest to enquiry converts. Reach does not equal revenue.
  • Lean into the genuine differentiators: authority, real outcomes, and Dr Gina on camera.
  • Align efforts so we complement, rather than duplicate, the work already underway with Marianne.

Your direction

  • What would success look like online over the next 6 to 12 months?
  • Who is the ideal patient you most want to attract?
  • How do you feel on camera, and how much time could you give to content?
  • How would you like us to work alongside Marianne?
  • What brand assets do you have (logo, fonts, photography, tone guide)?
