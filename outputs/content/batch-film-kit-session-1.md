# 🎬 Batch Film Kit — Session 1
## Film 4 reels in ~25 minutes. Prop your phone up. Read the prompts. Done.

**The deal:** you capture raw footage once. I do everything after — cut, captions, covers, music brief, virality check. This is how you pump out daily content without living in your phone.

**What we're filming:** Reels 1, 8, 9, 16 — your strongest hooks + proof (full scripts in `scripts/builder-reels-batch-1.md` & `-batch-2.md`).

---

## Before you hit record (5 min setup)

- **Camera:** phone (vertical, 9:16) propped at eye height, OR your Sony ZV-E1. Either is fine — real beats polished.
- **Light:** face a window. Natural light. No ring light (that's the look we're avoiding).
- **Setting:** deck / garden / bright corner with the coast or a clean background. Your world, not an office.
- **Wardrobe:** navy / white / cream / sand — something you'd actually wear. Not corporate.
- **Audio:** quiet room, or pop in earbuds with a mic. Sound matters more than picture.
- **Look:** straight down the lens. Talk to it like it's a mate across the table.

> Don't aim for perfect. A stumble is fine. Real is the brand. If you fluff a line, pause and say it again — I'll cut it clean.

---

## Part A — Talking head (film these back to back, ~15 min)

For each: say the **hook** straight down the lens, then the lines. Do 2 takes of each hook (I'll pick the best). Don't memorise — glance at the prompt, then say it like you mean it.

### 🎥 Clip 1 — Reel 1 · "The AI that writes my content"
- HOOK (2 takes): *"I haven't stared at a blank content page in three months. This is why."*
- THEN: *"Every morning, an AI I built reads the day's news, pulls what my audience cares about, and writes me a post — in my voice. I didn't write a line of code. I described what I wanted in plain English. That's it. This is one of about a dozen skills running my business — and I'll give you the whole sheet."*
- END LINE: *"Comment SKILLS and I'll send it to you."*

### 🎥 Clip 2 — Reel 9 · founder story
- HOOK (2 takes): *"Eighteen months ago I couldn't open a terminal. Today my business runs on AI I built myself. Here's the honest version."*
- THEN: *"I'm not a developer. I'm a yoga teacher who started a business and got buried in admin. So I started describing what I wanted to an AI and letting it build. Clumsy at first — then it clicked. Now content, sales, ops — big chunks run themselves. Same non-technical me. Just a new skill: knowing what to ask for."*
- END LINE: *"Comment SKILLS for the exact set I built."*

### 🎥 Clip 3 — Reel 8 · "my entire skills sheet, free" (open + close only — middle is screen-record)
- HOOK (2 takes): *"Here's every AI skill running my business — and I'm giving you the whole sheet, free."*
- CLOSE: *"I'm not gatekeeping this. Comment the word SKILLS and it's in your DMs."*

### 🎥 Clip 4 — Reel 16 · "this is my whole business, running itself" (voiceover reel — record audio + a short open)
- OPEN (film your face, 2 takes): *"This is my whole business. On one screen. Running itself."*
- VOICEOVER (just talk, I'll lay it over the screen-record): *"Content engine. Audit machine. Daily brief. Sales follow-up. Every one of these is a skill I built — and I can't code. This is what 'AI running your business' actually looks like. Not a chatbot. A system. Comment SKILLS and I'll DM you the whole sheet."*

## Part B — Screen recordings (~10 min)

Open Claude Code / your workspace and screen-record these (phone screen-record or QuickTime). Just capture the screen — no need to talk, I'll add your voiceover.

1. **Type `/content` and let it run** — the agents working, files appearing. (for Reel 1)
2. **Scroll slowly through your `.claude` folder** — the commands + agents list, then open one file so real skill names show. (for Reels 8 + 16)
3. **Open a finished daily brief or the audit PDF** — something real on screen. (b-roll for several)

> Blur or skip anything private (client names, keys). 20-30 seconds each is plenty.
